Metadata and Catalogue

In this page we provide both ODOHTEU catalogue for the datasets we have created and thirteen tables, one for each dataset, reporting metadata in a human-readable way; since chose to include metadata for the entire catalogue, but also separately for each dataset. We provided data with its metadata in order to provide effective reusable and interoperable data, adopting the DCAT AP version 2.0.0 specification and we chose Turtle as the serialization.

The need for metadata in rdf derives from the goal of developing a project that was compliant with LOD characteristics: from the use of open and machine-readable formats for the datasets to the specification of the license. Following the 5-STAR OPEN DATA MODEL, we have used meaningful, persistent, dereferenceable and unambiguous; as well as supported by a reliable infrastructure

Detected Victims 2013-14

Identifier https://odohteu.github.io/data/zip/2013-14EU-victims-exploitation-age-gender.zip
Title ODOHTEU - 2013-2014 victims by exploitation form, age and gender
Description This dataset contains data from Europen Union Commission about victims of human traffic in 2013 and 2014 as the result of the first anti-trafficking report by the institution. The dataset contains information regarding the total number of victims, the forms of exploitation, the age combined to the gender (thus the share of men, boys, girls and women). The spatial coverage of the dataset are the European Union member states.

Detected victims by citizenship 2015-2016

Identifier https://odohteu.github.io/data/zip/2013-14EU-victims-exploitation-age-gender.zip
Title ODOHTEU - 2015-2016 citizenship of EU victims
Description This dataset contains data from Europen Union Commission about victims of human traffic in 2015 and 2016 as the result of the Data collection on trafficking in human beings in the EU, report of 2018. The dataset contains information regarding the citizenship of registered victims in the European Union member states. Specifically, are reported only number of victims detected in European Union member states whose citizenship is of one of the countries of the European Union. The spatial coverage of the dataset are the European Union member states.
